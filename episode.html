  • • Lunar Regolith (Moon Dust)

01

00:00 - 10:00 | The Breach

"The Martian" Moment: Problem Identification

The alarm sounds. No sci-fi explosions—just the eerie, high-pitched hiss of a pressure leak. Host/Lead Engineer Kira (14) explains the physics: Why can't we just patch it? Because the CO2 levels are climbing faster than the O2 is leaking. If they don't scrub the air, they fall asleep in 30 minutes and never wake up.

02

10:00 - 25:00 | The "Bust" Labs

"Mythbusters" Moment: Testing the Regolith

The crew attempts to use raw Moon dust to filter the air.

THE EXPERIMENT: On-screen, we see a "Terrestrial Lab" (Earth-based experts) running a side-by-side test. They prove that raw regolith is too abrasive—it shreds the fan blades in seconds. MYTH BUSTED.

03

25:00 - 40:00 | Engineering Under Fire

"24" Moment: The Clock is Ticking

Oxygen at 12%. Kira realizes they can use "Sintering"—using the habitat's microwave heater to melt the regolith into a porous ceramic filter. It’s a race to calibrate the power levels without blowing the hab's circuit breaker.

04

40:00 - 45:00 | Success or Failure?

The homemade scrubber is installed. The crew waits in silence. The CO2 sensor flickers. 4.0%... 3.8%... 2.5%... **Safe.** The episode ends not with a party, but with a debrief on the *actual* NASA tech being developed for this exact scenario.
